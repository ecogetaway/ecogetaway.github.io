Open Source Infrastructure Research

I study the infrastructure gaps in scalability, inclusiveness, sustainability, and governance across open source ecosystems.

§01

Research Mission

Open source software has become the foundation of modern technology, yet many aspects of its infrastructure continue to evolve organically rather than by design. My research focuses on identifying recurring infrastructure gaps that affect the scalability, inclusiveness, sustainability, and governance of open source ecosystems.

Rather than proposing isolated tools, I study patterns across multiple projects to develop practical, evidence-based approaches that help maintainers, contributors, foundations, platform providers, and the broader open source community.

  1. Study real-world open source projects.
  2. Identify recurring infrastructure gaps.
  3. Analyze existing community practices.
  4. Develop practical reference implementations.
  5. Publish findings through articles, repositories, and community discussions.
